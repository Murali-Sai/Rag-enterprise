ACME FINANCIAL HOLDINGS — CREDIT RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy governs the identification, measurement, monitoring, and mitigation of credit risk arising from lending activities, derivatives trading, securities financing transactions, and counterparty exposures across all business segments.

2. CREDIT RISK APPETITE

The Board has established the following credit risk appetite metrics:
- Maximum single-name exposure: 5% of Tier 1 Capital ($3.4B)
- Maximum industry concentration: 15% of total credit exposure
- Expected loss rate (through-the-cycle): < 75 bps
- Non-performing loan ratio target: < 1.5%
- Allowance coverage ratio: minimum 1.5x NPL

3. CREDIT RATING FRAMEWORK

3.1 Internal Rating Scale
- Rating 1-3: Investment Grade (Probability of Default < 0.5%)
- Rating 4-5: Non-Investment Grade (PD 0.5% - 3.0%)
- Rating 6-7: Substandard (PD 3.0% - 10.0%)
- Rating 8: Doubtful (PD 10.0% - 50.0%)
- Rating 9: Loss (PD > 50.0%)
- Rating 10: Default

3.2 Rating Methodology
- Corporate obligors: Quantitative scorecard (financial ratios, cash flow analysis) combined with qualitative assessment (industry, management, competitive position)
- Financial institutions: Peer analysis, regulatory standing, funding profile
- Sovereign: Macro indicators, fiscal metrics, political risk assessment
- Retail: Behavioral scoring, bureau data, payment history

4. CREDIT APPROVAL AUTHORITY

4.1 Delegated Authority Limits
- Relationship Manager: up to $5M
- Credit Officer: up to $25M
- Senior Credit Officer: up to $100M
- Regional Credit Committee: up to $500M
- Enterprise Credit Committee: up to $1B
- Board Risk Committee: above $1B

4.2 Required Documentation
- Credit Application Memo (CAM)
- Financial Statement Analysis (minimum 3 years)
- Industry and Peer Analysis
- Collateral Valuation (if applicable)
- Environmental and Social Risk Assessment
- Compliance and KYC Clearance

5. PORTFOLIO MANAGEMENT

5.1 Concentration Limits
- Top 10 Exposures: Maximum 20% of total exposure
- Single Country (non-US): Maximum 10% of total exposure
- Single Industry: Maximum 15% of total exposure
- Commercial Real Estate: Maximum 12% of total exposure (currently at 10.9%)

5.2 Current Portfolio Composition (as of Q4 2024)
- Investment Grade: 72% of total exposure
- Non-Investment Grade: 23%
- Substandard and Below: 5%
- Geographic: US 65%, Europe 18%, Asia-Pacific 12%, Other 5%
- Sector: Financial Institutions 22%, Technology 14%, Healthcare 11%, Energy 9%, CRE 11%, Consumer 10%, Industrial 8%, Other 15%

6. EARLY WARNING AND WATCHLIST

6.1 Early Warning Indicators
- Financial covenant breach or near-breach
- Credit rating downgrade (internal or external)
- Significant decline in market-implied credit spreads
- Material adverse change in obligor's business
- Industry-wide stress events

6.2 Watchlist Categories
- Watch: Enhanced monitoring, quarterly review
- Substandard: Active remediation plan required
- Doubtful: Workout strategy, specific reserve required
- Loss: Write-off recommendation

7. LOSS GIVEN DEFAULT (LGD) AND RECOVERY

- Senior Secured: Expected LGD 25-35%
- Senior Unsecured: Expected LGD 45-55%
- Subordinated: Expected LGD 65-75%
- Recovery process managed by Special Situations Group
- Average recovery rate (historical): 58% for corporate exposures

8. REGULATORY CAPITAL

- Standardized Approach: Used for regulatory reporting
- Advanced IRB: Used for internal risk management
- Risk-Weighted Assets (Credit Risk): $485B
- Expected Loss (EL): $2.8B annually
- Unexpected Loss (UL): Covered by CET1 capital allocation

9. CURRENT EXPECTED CREDIT LOSSES (CECL)

- Methodology: Discounted cash flow for individually assessed loans; probability-weighted forecast for pooled loans
- Macroeconomic Scenarios: Baseline (50% weight), Downside (30%), Severe Downside (15%), Upside (5%)
- Allowance for Credit Losses: $4.2B
- Day 1 Provision for New Originations: Based on lifetime expected loss
- Qualitative Adjustment: Up to 15% of modeled reserve for emerging risks
