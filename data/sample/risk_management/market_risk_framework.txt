ACME FINANCIAL HOLDINGS — MARKET RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK

1. OVERVIEW

The Market Risk Management Framework establishes the policies, methodologies, and governance structure for identifying, measuring, monitoring, and controlling market risk across all trading and banking book positions.

2. RISK GOVERNANCE

2.1 Three Lines of Defense
- First Line: Front Office / Business Units — own and manage risk-taking
- Second Line: Market Risk Management (independent) — sets limits, monitors, and challenges
- Third Line: Internal Audit — provides independent assurance

2.2 Committee Structure
- Board Risk Committee: Quarterly review of risk appetite and framework
- Enterprise Risk Committee (ERC): Monthly review of risk metrics and breaches
- Market Risk Committee (MRC): Weekly review of trading book risk
- Desk-Level Risk Reviews: Daily P&L and risk metric review

3. VALUE-AT-RISK (VaR)

3.1 Methodology
- Model: Historical Simulation VaR
- Confidence Level: 99% (1-day) and 99% (10-day)
- Lookback Period: 2 years of historical data, with equal weighting
- Holding Period: 1-day for trading book, 10-day for regulatory capital

3.2 VaR Limits
- Firm-wide 99% 1-day VaR Limit: $100M
- Average Daily VaR (2024): $62M
- Maximum Daily VaR (2024): $89M (occurred during March volatility event)
- Minimum Daily VaR (2024): $41M

3.3 VaR by Risk Factor (Average 2024):
- Interest Rate Risk: $28M
- Equity Risk: $22M
- Credit Spread Risk: $18M
- Foreign Exchange Risk: $12M
- Commodity Risk: $8M
- Diversification Benefit: ($26M)
- Total VaR: $62M

4. STRESS TESTING

4.1 Regulatory Stress Tests
- CCAR/DFAST: Annual submission to Federal Reserve
- Scenarios: Baseline, Adverse, Severely Adverse
- 2024 DFAST Results (Severely Adverse):
  - Projected Losses: $18.2B over 9 quarters
  - Minimum CET1 Ratio: 8.4% (vs. 4.5% minimum)
  - Pre-provision Net Revenue (Stressed): $12.8B

4.2 Internal Stress Tests
- Historical Scenarios: 2008 Financial Crisis, 2020 COVID, 2022 Rate Shock, 2023 Banking Crisis
- Hypothetical Scenarios: Geopolitical shock, inflation spiral, liquidity freeze, cyber event
- Reverse Stress Tests: Identify scenarios that would breach capital thresholds

4.3 Sensitivity Analysis
- DV01 (Dollar Value of 1bp): $8.2M across the yield curve
- CS01 (Credit Spread 01): $5.1M
- Equity Delta: $42M per 1% move in S&P 500
- Vega: $12M per 1 vol point across equity surfaces

5. RISK LIMITS FRAMEWORK

5.1 Limit Hierarchy
- Board Risk Appetite → Firm-Level Limits → Division Limits → Desk Limits → Trader Limits
- All limits are hard limits; breaches require immediate escalation

5.2 Limit Types
- VaR Limits (1-day and 10-day)
- Stress Loss Limits (per scenario)
- Sensitivity Limits (DV01, CS01, delta, vega, gamma)
- Notional Limits (gross and net)
- Concentration Limits (single name, sector, geography)
- Loss Limits (daily, monthly, quarterly stop-loss)

5.3 Breach Escalation
- Level 1 (Warning at 80%): Notification to desk head and risk manager
- Level 2 (Soft Breach at 100%): Escalation to division head; plan to remediate within 2 days
- Level 3 (Hard Breach at 120%): Immediate escalation to CRO; forced position reduction

6. COUNTERPARTY CREDIT RISK

6.1 Exposure Measurement
- Current Exposure: Mark-to-market value of derivatives portfolio
- Potential Future Exposure (PFE): 97.5th percentile at 1-year horizon
- Credit Valuation Adjustment (CVA): $890M as of year-end
- Wrong-Way Risk: Monitored through correlation analysis between exposure and counterparty credit

6.2 Collateral Management
- Variation Margin: Daily exchange under CSA agreements
- Initial Margin: Calculated using ISDA SIMM methodology
- Eligible Collateral: Cash (USD, EUR, GBP, JPY), US Treasuries, G7 sovereign bonds
- Rehypothecation: Permitted up to 140% of client debit balances

7. MODEL RISK MANAGEMENT

- All pricing and risk models subject to independent validation
- Annual model inventory review covering 850+ models
- Model Risk Rating: Tier 1 (critical), Tier 2 (significant), Tier 3 (standard)
- Tier 1 models validated annually; Tier 2 every 18 months; Tier 3 every 2 years
- Model limitations documented and communicated to users
