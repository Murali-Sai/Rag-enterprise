PART I

Item 1. Business

ACME Financial Holdings, Inc. ("ACME" or the "Company") is a global financial services firm and a bank holding company. The Company's principal activities include investment banking, sales and trading of securities and derivatives, asset management, prime brokerage, and commercial banking.

The Company operates through four principal business segments:
1. Institutional Securities — includes investment banking (M&A advisory, equity and debt underwriting), sales and trading (equities, FICC), and research
2. Wealth Management — includes financial advisory and brokerage services to individual investors
3. Asset Management — includes management of pooled vehicles, separate accounts, and fund of funds
4. Commercial Banking — includes corporate lending, commercial real estate, and treasury services

Revenue by Segment (FY 2024):
- Institutional Securities: $18.2B (48% of total revenue)
- Wealth Management: $11.5B (30%)
- Asset Management: $5.1B (13%)
- Commercial Banking: $3.4B (9%)

Total Net Revenue: $38.2B
Net Income: $9.1B
Return on Equity (ROE): 14.8%
Return on Tangible Common Equity (ROTCE): 17.2%

Item 1A. Risk Factors

Market Risk: The Company is exposed to market risk from changes in interest rates, equity prices, foreign exchange rates, and commodity prices. The Company's daily Value-at-Risk (VaR) averaged $62 million during 2024.

Credit Risk: As of December 31, 2024, the Company's total credit exposure was $385 billion. Non-performing assets represented 0.8% of total assets. The allowance for credit losses was $4.2 billion, representing a coverage ratio of 2.1x.

Liquidity Risk: The Company maintained a Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) of 118%, exceeding the regulatory minimum of 100%. The Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR) was 112%.

Operational Risk: The Company faces operational risk from internal processes, people, systems, and external events. Operational risk capital under Basel III Advanced Approach was $12.4 billion.

Regulatory Risk: The Company is subject to extensive regulation by federal and state agencies including the Federal Reserve, OCC, SEC, CFTC, and FINRA. Basel III capital requirements mandate a minimum CET1 ratio of 4.5% plus applicable buffers. The Company's CET1 ratio was 13.2% as of year-end.

Item 7. Management's Discussion and Analysis

Capital Markets Revenue:
Total trading revenue was $14.8B, driven by strong performance in Equities (+15% YoY) and FICC (+8% YoY). The equities division benefited from higher client activity in derivatives and prime brokerage. FICC performance was led by credit products and macro trading.

Investment Banking:
Advisory revenue was $3.2B, reflecting a recovery in M&A volumes. Equity underwriting revenue of $2.1B increased 22% driven by IPO activity. Debt underwriting revenue was $2.8B, supported by leveraged finance and investment-grade issuance.

Net Interest Income:
Net interest income was $8.4B, up 5% YoY. Net interest margin was 2.15%, compared to 2.08% in the prior year. The increase was primarily driven by higher rates on the lending portfolio, partially offset by higher deposit costs.

Provision for Credit Losses:
The provision for credit losses was $1.8B, up from $1.2B in the prior year. The increase reflected deterioration in the commercial real estate portfolio and a modest increase in consumer credit delinquencies.

Compensation and Benefits:
Total compensation and benefits expense was $15.6B, representing a compensation ratio of 40.8% of net revenue. The ratio decreased from 42.1% in the prior year, reflecting operating leverage.
