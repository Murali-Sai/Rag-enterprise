ACME FINANCIAL HOLDINGS — REGULATORY COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

1. REGULATORY LANDSCAPE

ACME Financial Holdings is subject to oversight by multiple regulatory authorities:

1.1 Primary Regulators
- Federal Reserve Board (FRB): Consolidated supervision of the bank holding company
- Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC): Supervision of the national bank subsidiary
- Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC): Regulation of broker-dealer and investment adviser subsidiaries
- Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC): Regulation of swap dealer activities
- Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA): Self-regulatory oversight of broker-dealer

1.2 Key Regulatory Frameworks
- Bank Holding Company Act / Regulation Y
- Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act
- Volcker Rule (Section 619 of Dodd-Frank)
- Basel III Capital and Liquidity Requirements
- Bank Secrecy Act / Anti-Money Laundering (BSA/AML)
- Securities Exchange Act of 1934
- Investment Advisers Act of 1940
- Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (Privacy)
- Community Reinvestment Act (CRA)

2. VOLCKER RULE COMPLIANCE

2.1 Prohibited Activities
- Proprietary trading in securities, derivatives, and commodity futures
- Ownership interests in covered funds (hedge funds, private equity funds)
- Certain transactions with covered funds (extensions of credit, guarantees)

2.2 Permitted Activities
- Market making: Inventory positions that are near-term customer demand driven
- Underwriting: Positions related to distribution of securities
- Hedging: Risk-mitigating hedging activity directly related to identified risks
- Trading in government obligations (US Treasuries, agency securities)
- Foreign exchange facilitating customer transactions

2.3 Compliance Program
- Metrics-based monitoring of trading desk inventory, revenue, and risk
- Daily reporting of RENTD (Reasonably Expected Near-Term Demand) metrics
- Quarterly CEO attestation to Federal Reserve
- Independent testing by Compliance and Internal Audit

3. ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) AND SANCTIONS

3.1 Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Standard CDD: All new accounts — identity verification, beneficial ownership
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD): High-risk customers — PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, correspondent banks
- Ongoing monitoring: Transaction monitoring, periodic reviews

3.2 KYC Requirements
- Customer Identification Program (CIP): Government-issued ID, verification
- Beneficial Ownership: 25% ownership threshold identification
- Source of Funds / Source of Wealth: Required for EDD customers
- Periodic Review: Annual for high-risk, biennial for medium, triennial for standard

3.3 Sanctions Compliance
- OFAC screening: All customers, counterparties, and transactions
- Real-time screening of wire transfers
- Sanctions list updates within 24 hours of publication
- Escalation: Any potential match reviewed within 4 hours

3.4 Suspicious Activity Reporting
- Transaction monitoring system covers $2.1T in annual transaction volume
- Alert disposition: Within 30 days of generation
- SAR filing: Within 30 days of determination (60 days with extension)
- 2024 Statistics: 12,500 alerts generated, 2,800 investigations, 850 SARs filed

4. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

4.1 Information Barriers (Chinese Walls)
- Physical separation between Investment Banking and Research
- Physical separation between Investment Banking and Sales & Trading
- Above-the-wall lists maintained for active engagements
- Wall crossings require Compliance approval and are logged

4.2 Personal Trading
- Pre-clearance required for all personal securities transactions
- 30-day holding period for equity positions
- Restricted list: No trading in securities of companies on the restricted list
- Quarterly holdings reporting and annual certification

4.3 Material Non-Public Information (MNPI)
- MNPI may not be used for trading or shared outside information barriers
- Employees receiving MNPI must notify Compliance immediately
- Grey list maintained for securities where MNPI may exist
- Trading surveillance monitors for potential insider trading patterns

5. CONDUCT RISK

5.1 Market Conduct
- No market manipulation (spoofing, layering, wash trading)
- Best execution obligation for client orders
- Fair pricing of OTC derivatives
- Trade reporting: All reportable transactions within regulatory timeframes

5.2 Sales Practices
- Suitability assessment before recommending products
- Reg BI (Best Interest) compliance for retail recommendations
- Clear and fair disclosure of fees, risks, and conflicts
- Product governance: New product approval process with risk assessment

6. DATA PRIVACY AND PROTECTION

- Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act: Privacy notices, opt-out rights, safeguarding requirements
- GDPR: For EU client data — consent management, data subject rights, DPO appointed
- CCPA/CPRA: For California resident data
- Data Classification: Restricted, Confidential, Internal, Public
- Encryption: AES-256 for data at rest, TLS 1.3 for data in transit
- Data Retention: Per regulatory requirements (7 years for trade records, 5 years for communications)

7. REGULATORY EXAMINATIONS AND REPORTING

7.1 Regular Filings
- FR Y-9C (Consolidated Financial Statements): Quarterly
- FFIEC 101 (Basel III Advanced Approaches): Quarterly
- CCAR/DFAST Submissions: Annual
- SEC Form 10-K/10-Q: Annual and Quarterly
- FOCUS Report (Broker-Dealer): Monthly

7.2 Examination Readiness
- Dedicated Regulatory Affairs team managing 15+ concurrent examinations
- MRA (Matters Requiring Attention) tracking: Currently 12 open MRAs
- MRIA (Matters Requiring Immediate Attention): 2 open, both with remediation plans
