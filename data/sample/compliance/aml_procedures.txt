ACME FINANCIAL HOLDINGS — AML/KYC PROCEDURES MANUAL

1. CUSTOMER ONBOARDING PROCEDURES

1.1 Account Opening
Step 1: Collect Customer Identification Program (CIP) documents
  - Individuals: Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license), date of birth, SSN/TIN, residential address
  - Entities: Certificate of incorporation, articles of organization, EIN, registered address, operating agreement
  - Trusts: Trust agreement, identification of trustees and beneficiaries

Step 2: Beneficial Ownership Identification
  - Identify all individuals owning 25% or more of the legal entity
  - Identify one individual with significant management control
  - Collect CIP documentation for all identified beneficial owners

Step 3: Risk Rating Assignment
  - Low Risk: US-based individuals, publicly traded companies, regulated financial institutions
  - Medium Risk: Private companies, non-US persons from standard-risk jurisdictions
  - High Risk: PEPs, MSBs, non-US entities from high-risk jurisdictions, shell companies, cryptocurrency businesses

Step 4: Screening
  - OFAC/SDN list screening
  - Global sanctions screening (EU, UK, UN)
  - PEP database screening
  - Adverse media screening
  - Negative news check

Step 5: Enhanced Due Diligence (if High Risk)
  - Source of funds documentation
  - Source of wealth documentation
  - Senior management approval required
  - Expected transaction activity profiling
  - Annual review cycle

2. TRANSACTION MONITORING

2.1 Automated Monitoring System
- Platform: Actimize NICE
- Transaction Types Monitored:
  - Wire transfers (domestic and international)
  - ACH transactions
  - Check deposits
  - Securities transactions
  - Cash transactions above $10,000 (CTR filing)
  - Structured transactions below $10,000 (potential structuring)

2.2 Alert Typologies
- Rapid movement of funds (pass-through activity)
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile
- Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions (FATF grey/black list)
- Unusual wire transfer patterns
- Round-dollar transactions
- Layering patterns in securities trading
- Funnel accounts
- Cash structuring patterns
- Shell company indicators
- Trade-based money laundering indicators

2.3 Alert Disposition Process
- Level 1 Analyst: Initial review and triage (5 business days)
- Level 2 Analyst: Detailed investigation (15 business days)
- Level 3 / SAR Decision: Determination and filing (10 business days)
- Quality Assurance: 10% random sample review by QA team

3. SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORTING

3.1 SAR Filing Requirements
- File within 30 calendar days of initial detection
- Extension to 60 days if no suspect identified
- Continuing activity: Subsequent SARs every 90 days
- Joint SARs with FinCEN for cases involving multiple institutions

3.2 SAR Content
- Subject information (if known)
- Suspicious activity type (money laundering, fraud, structuring, terrorist financing, etc.)
- Narrative: Clear description of the suspicious activity, including dates, amounts, and patterns
- Supporting documentation retained for 5 years from filing date

3.3 SAR Confidentiality
- SARs are strictly confidential
- No disclosure to the subject of the SAR (tipping off prohibition)
- Limited internal access on a need-to-know basis
- Safe harbor protection for good-faith filings

4. SANCTIONS COMPLIANCE

4.1 Real-Time Screening
- All wire transfers screened against OFAC SDN list in real time
- Potential matches generate automatic holds
- Compliance team reviews within 4 hours
- True matches: Block transaction, file blocking report with OFAC within 10 business days
- False positives: Release with documentation

4.2 Prohibited Jurisdictions
- Comprehensively sanctioned: Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Crimea region
- Sectoral sanctions: Russia, Venezuela (specific sectors)
- Enhanced screening: High-risk jurisdictions per FATF recommendations

5. RECORDKEEPING

- CIP records: 5 years after account closure
- Transaction records: 5 years from date of transaction
- SAR records: 5 years from filing date
- Wire transfer records (>$3,000): 5 years
- CTR records: 5 years from filing date
- Training records: 5 years
- All records must be retrievable within 48 hours of regulatory request

6. TRAINING

- All employees: Annual AML awareness training (mandatory)
- Customer-facing staff: Enhanced AML training including red flags and escalation procedures
- Compliance team: Specialized training on typologies, investigations, and regulatory updates
- Board of Directors: Annual AML risk briefing
- Training completion rate target: 100% within 30 days of assignment
- 2024 completion rate: 99.2%
