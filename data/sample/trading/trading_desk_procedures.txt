ACME FINANCIAL HOLDINGS — TRADING DESK PROCEDURES AND CONTROLS

1. PRE-TRADE CONTROLS

1.1 Order Validation
- All orders validated against pre-trade risk limits before execution
- Fat finger checks: Orders exceeding 5x average daily volume or $50M notional require manual confirmation
- Price reasonableness checks: Reject orders deviating >5% from last traded price
- Credit limit checks: Real-time counterparty exposure validation
- Restricted list check: Automated screening against compliance restricted list

1.2 Trading Limits
Daily P&L Stop-Loss Limits:
- Desk Level: Varies by desk ($5M - $25M)
- Individual Trader: $1M - $5M (based on seniority and mandate)
- Monthly Stop-Loss: 3x daily limit
- Quarterly Stop-Loss: 5x daily limit

Position Limits:
- Gross Notional: Per desk, reviewed quarterly
- Net Delta: Per trader, monitored in real-time
- Concentration: No single position >10% of desk notional
- End-of-day inventory limits for market-making desks

1.3 Approved Products
- All products must go through New Product Approval (NPA) process
- Approved Product List maintained by Risk Management
- Complex/exotic products require enhanced review and additional capital
- Structured products: Pre-approval from Structuring Compliance

2. TRADE EXECUTION

2.1 Best Execution
- Obligation to seek best execution for all client orders
- Factors: Price, speed, likelihood of execution, settlement, size
- Venue analysis: Quarterly review of execution quality across venues
- Time-stamping: All order events logged with microsecond precision

2.2 Order Handling
- Client orders executed promptly and fairly
- Priority: Client orders before proprietary orders
- Information barriers between market-making and proprietary activities
- Block trades: Fair allocation per allocation policy
- Aggregation: Permitted if beneficial to all participating accounts

2.3 Electronic Trading
- Algorithmic trading subject to additional controls
- Kill switch capability: Ability to cancel all open orders within 1 second
- Maximum order rate: 1,000 orders/second per trading system
- Automated market quality checks: Spread, depth, volatility monitoring
- Quarterly review of algorithm performance and behavior

3. POST-TRADE PROCESSES

3.1 Trade Confirmation
- T+0: All trades confirmed by end of business day
- Electronic confirmations via DTCC CTM, Markitwire, or bilateral
- Unconfirmed trades escalated after T+1
- Aging report reviewed daily by Operations and Trading

3.2 Settlement
- Equities: T+1 settlement (US, effective May 2024)
- Fixed Income: T+1 or T+2 depending on product
- OTC Derivatives: Per ISDA Master Agreement
- FX Spot: T+2 (CLS settlement for major pairs)
- Settlement fails tracked daily; penalties per CSDR (EU) and SEC Rule 15c6-1

3.3 Reconciliation
- Daily position reconciliation: Front office vs. back office vs. custodian
- Daily cash reconciliation: Nostro accounts reconciled by 10 AM
- P&L reconciliation: Trading P&L vs. official P&L reconciled daily
- Breaks >$100K escalated to desk head and Operations management

4. TRADE SURVEILLANCE

4.1 Monitored Behaviors
- Spoofing and layering
- Wash trading
- Front-running and information misuse
- Cross-product manipulation
- Marking the close / marking the open
- Window dressing
- Insider trading indicators

4.2 Surveillance Systems
- Trade surveillance system: NICE Actimize SURVEIL-X
- Communication surveillance: Voice, email, chat, Bloomberg messages
- Cross-market correlation analysis
- Pattern detection using machine learning models
- Alert triage: T+1 for critical, T+5 for standard

5. END-OF-DAY PROCEDURES

5.1 Daily Checklist
- All trades booked and confirmed
- P&L explained and signed off by desk head
- Position limits verified (no overnight breaches)
- Risk reports reviewed (VaR, Greeks, stress)
- New issue allocations completed
- Trade breaks resolved or escalated
- Restricted list violations checked and cleared
- Manager sign-off on daily risk report by 6:30 PM

5.2 Reporting
- Daily P&L report to desk head and division management
- Daily risk report to Market Risk Management
- Trade blotter submitted to Compliance
- Position report to Operations for settlement processing
